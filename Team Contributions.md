## Team Contributions

**Griff Berry**

Griff served as the project's researcher, Red Team Leader, and co-final reviewer. He was

responsible for helping brainstorm the project question, and authored the 

"Question and Background Information" section. In addition to helping develop the

central research question, he provided context on the dataset and why the question

is worth investigating. This helped establish the group's hypotheses around hours

studied, attendance, and exam anxiety as the primary lifestyle predictors of

academic performance.

For the Red Team's evaluation phase, Griff led the full linear model analysis. He created

the dummy variable regression walkthrough, which demonstrated how categorical variables

relate to group means, and then compared model performance across numeric-only,

categorical-only, combined, and polynomial configurations. He also ran the

final held-out test set evaluation. He interpreted the predicted vs. actual

and residual plots, and performed the coefficient analysis that directly

answered the project's research question. This was done through identifying which lifestyle factors

carried the strongest positive and negative associations with Final_Score.

Griff was also the author of the project's "conclusions" section. He explained the results across

all modeling stages, evaluating the hypotheses against what the data actually

showed, and clearly communicating the model's limitations. This included the

unexplained variance, the observational nature of the data, and the model's

reduced reliability for lower-performing students.
